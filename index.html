Hoa Hồng – Biểu Tượng Của Cái Đẹp

Hoa hồng – Biểu tượng vượt thời gian của tình yêu và sự trù phú

One of the most loved flowers, the rose is symbol of beauty, love, peace and the lushness of nature. These prickly shrubs with their fragrant flowers have been around for about 35 million years. People began cultivating roses in Asia 5,000 years ago, and since then, thousands of varieties have been developed.


Là một trong những loài hoa được yêu thích nhất, hoa hồng là biểu tượng của vẻ đẹp, tình yêu, hòa bình và sự trù phú của thiên nhiên. Những bụi cây có gai với những bông hoa thơm ngát này đã tồn tại khoảng 35 triệu năm. Con người bắt đầu trồng hoa hồng ở châu Á 5.000 năm trước, và từ đó, hàng ngàn giống loài đã được phát triển.

📖 In a Word — Từ Nguyên

The word "rose" came into use in Old English, and can trace its roots to the Latin rosa.


It may be derived from the Greek word "rhodon".

🌸 Anatomy 101 — Cấu Tạo Hoa Hồng

🌺

Corolla & Petals — Tràng hoa & Cánh hoa

The corolla is the whorl of petals. This is the colorful, fragrant part of the flower.

🌿

Sepal & Penduncle — Lá đài & Cuống hoa

Sepals protect the bud before it opens. The penduncle is the stem of the flower.

✨

Anther & Filament — Bao phấn & Chỉ nhị

Parts of the stamen responsible for producing and supporting pollen.

🍓

Rose Hip — Quả giả (Quả hồng)

The part left on the plant after blooming. Rich in Vitamin C, used to help manage diabetes and boost immunity.

🗡️

Prickle (Thorn) — Gai

Sickle-shaped hooks that help the rose climb and hang onto other vegetation when growing over it.

🍃

Leaflets & Stipule — Lá chét & Lá kèm

Compound leaves are made up of several leaflets. Leaflets usually have a serrated (toothed) edge.

🏛️ Looking Back — Lịch sử & Thần thoại

🏛️ Ancient Times

Greeks: Used roses as accessories. On festive occasions, they would adorn themselves with garlands of roses.

Romans: Legend says white roses grew where the tears of Venus (goddess of love) fell as she mourned Adonis. Cupid gave Harpocrates (god of silence) a rose to keep her secrets.

Egypt: Cleopatra used rose fragrance and covered banquet tables and floors in rose petals when entertaining Mark Antony.

⚔️ Religion & Wars

Early Christianity: The five petals symbolized the five wounds of Christ, and the red color was the blood of martyrs. The rose is associated with the Virgin Mary.

War of the Roses (1455-1485): A series of civil wars over the throne of England. The house of Lancaster used the red rose symbol, and the house of York used a white rose.

🌹 Rose Types — Phân Loại Hoa Hồng

⏳ Old Garden Roses

  • Also known as antique or heritage roses.
  • Grown prior to 1867.
  • Some date back to the ancient Romans.
  • Known for their beautiful fragrance and variety of colors.

🆕 Modern Roses

  • Hybrid tea roses: Have a single blossom on a long stem; a favorite for cut flowers.
  • Floribunda roses: Produce abundant clusters of small flowers.
  • Climbing roses: Mutations that produce long canes that ramble over trees or supports.

💡 Did You Know? — Bạn Có Biết?

Shakespeare refers to roses more than 50 times in his writings.
The great Chinese philosopher Confucius is said to have had 600 books on caring for roses.
The world's oldest living rose bush is thought to be 1,000 years old (Hildesheim Cathedral in Germany).
Napoleon's wife, Josephine, grew more than 250 varieties of roses.
Rose hips are rich in Vitamin C, A, D, and E. They can be used to make jams and teas.
Samuel de Champlain brought the first cultivated roses to North America in the 17th century.
Belinda's Dream was first cultivated in 1992 (a cross between Tiffany and Jersey Beauty).
The Othello rose is a hybrid variety with large red flowers that slowly turn purple with age.

✨ Wild Roses & DIY Perfume

🌿 True Wild Roses

True wild roses have exactly five petals and occur naturally. They are native to North America, Europe and Asia.

The wild rose produces fragrant flowers once a year in spring. It is the original ancestor of all rose varieties.

Note: Fungal diseases may cause yellowing leaves. Spider mites like to feed on the leaves.

🧪 Rose Petal Perfume Recipe

  • Step 1: Bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Turn off heat.
  • Step 2: Add 2 cups of fresh rose petals and let steep until water is cool.
  • Step 3: Strain the water into a glass bowl. Squeeze liquid out of petals.
  • Step 4: Add a drop of red food coloring and essential rose oil. Stir.
  • Step 5: Pour into a clean bottle. Store in a cool, dark place (keeps for ~2 weeks).
